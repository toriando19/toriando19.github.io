DR - Danish Broadcast



DR (Danmarks Radio) is Denmark’s national public service broadcaster and one of the country’s most influential and respected media institutions. Founded in 1925, DR has played a vital role in shaping the Danish media landscape for nearly a century, delivering high-quality journalism, entertainment, culture, and educational content to audiences across the country.
As a publicly funded organization, DR operates under a mandate to serve the public interest by providing accessible, independent, and diverse programming. Its funding primarily comes through taxation, ensuring editorial independence and freedom from commercial influence.

Admin URL Interfaces

One of my first projects during the internship was developing an internal admin interface for managing URLs on dr.dk. As part of Team DR.DK – responsible for the structure and workflow of the main website – I built a tool that allowed users to add, view, and soft-delete page entries through a clean interface. The system included search and filtering options, along with metadata like status codes, dates, categories, and titles. Its main purpose was to help clean up the database by identifying and removing unused pages with a 400 status.

Alongside this, I developed a smaller, terminal-based version for external use. This version exposed only the core API endpoints – add, read, and delete – intended for DR employees who preferred interacting via command line.



DRDK Index – Dashboard

My last and final project during the internship was the development of the DRDK Index Dashboard – a centralized overview that brought together all the smaller projects I had worked on into one united interface. The dashboard served as a helpful control panel for the “dr.dk URL backend-universe”, offering both technical insights and strategic value.

It included a status overview of connections to the database, the internal URL interface, and the automated robots.txt maintenance system. I added a more detailed section for robots.txt data, including a graph of recent updates, counts of updates in the last 24 hours, URLs flagged for immediate updates, and deleted entries. A “Force Maintenance" button was also implemented to manually trigger the robots maintenance loop, which I had previously automated to run hourly. Additionally, I visualized search engine usage with daily graphs and created a tracker for the most searched keywords from the past 7 days – potentially useful for SEO strategy or content planning.



Bachelor-exam Project: MeDR

MeDR is a digital product solution developed with the aim of combating loneliness and strengthening community among young people in Denmark – in line with DR’s vision of fostering social unity. The solution functions as an integrated social media platform on dr.dk, where users are connected based on their interests and behavior across DR’s content. By drawing on DR’s rich media universe – including podcasts, TV series, and articles – MeDR creates a new way for people to meet and share experiences. Through content-based matching and social features, users are encouraged to discover communities, engage in conversations, and form new relationships based on personal preferences.

The prototype of this project was coded with a customized MEPN-stack: MongoDB, Express, PostgreSQL and NodeJS.



Scrumban

Besides the backend work, the internship also gave me valuable insight into project planning and team collaboration in a large organization like DR. We used a Scrumban approach, where meetings played a central role in aligning efforts – both across departments and within our own team, Team Platform/Enabler. Every step of the process had to be documented in the system to ensure transparency and consistency. We held regular meetings such as retrospectives, refinements, sprint planning, and daily stand-ups, all contributing to a clear, structured and efficient workflow.
